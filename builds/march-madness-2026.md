### 1. 🔵 MICHIGAN — Midwest #1 Seed (31-3)
**Why Dangerous:**
Michigan is the most complete team in the country. Dusty May assembled an absolutely stacked roster through the transfer portal: UAB transfer Yaxel Lendeborg (14.4 ppg, 7 rpg, 51% FG), elite point guard Elliot Cadeau, rim protector Morez Johnson, skilled big Aday Mara (7-3). The Wolverines have a top-10 offense AND the nation's most feared defense. They've won 10 games by 30+ points this season, seven by 40+. Won the Big Ten outright by 4 games. Most favorable path to the Final Four of any #1 seed — ranked #1 by Jeff Eisenberg's 1-68 rankings.

**Why Vulnerable:**
Lost their best perimeter shot creator LJ Cason to a knee injury in late February. Lost the Big Ten title game to Purdue on Sunday. Michigan is 179th nationally in turnover rate, which plays right into Iowa State's hands if they meet in the Elite Eight. Not battle-tested in close games lately.

---

### 2. 🔵 DUKE — East #1 Seed (32-2) | #1 Overall
**Why Dangerous:**
Cameron Boozer is the frontrunner for National Player of the Year — 22.5 ppg, 10.2 rpg, 4.2 apg, 1.5 spg, 56.5% FG, 40.9% from 3. A do-it-all, inside-out 6'9" force. Duke won the ACC regular season and tournament, went 17-1 in ACC play. Beat Michigan, Florida, UConn, and a series of elite opponents. Boozer will carry this team regardless of surrounding talent.

**Why Vulnerable:**
Starting center Patrick Ngongba is recovering from a foot injury (missed last two weeks, playing status unclear). Starting guard Caleb Foster underwent surgery for a fractured foot and is unlikely to return before the Final Four at the earliest. Duke runs a tight rotation — losing two starters in a single-elimination tournament is brutal. The East region is a murderer's row: UConn, Michigan State, Kansas, St. John's, all coached by Hall of Famers.

---

### 3. 🔴 ARIZONA — West #1 Seed (32-2)
**Why Dangerous:**
The most well-rounded team in college basketball. On a 9-game win streak coming into the tournament. Beat 12 ranked opponents this season — first team ever to do that before the NCAAs. Top-5 offense and top-5 defense. Deep enough to bring impact players like Anthony Dell'Orso and Tobe Awaka off the bench. Guard Brayden Burries (freshman, potential top-10 pick) is their leading scorer. Jaden Bradley hit a buzzer-beater in the Big 12 semis. Koa Peat dropped 21 in the Big 12 title win over Houston. Won Big 12 regular season AND tournament.

**Why Vulnerable:**
Arizona has NOT made the Final Four since 2001, losing five Elite Eight games in heartbreaking fashion in the 25 years since. That's a real curse to overcome. Arkansas (13-of-last-15 wins) and Darius Acuff Jr. await in a potential Sweet 16. Purdue is red-hot and looms as a potential Elite Eight opponent.

---

### 4. 🟠 FLORIDA — South #1 Seed (26-7) | Defending Champions
**Why Dangerous:**
Returns every key frontcourt player from last year's national championship team — Rueben Chinyelu, Thomas Haugh (17.1 ppg, team-high), and Alex Condon all back and better than ever. Haugh evolved from 6th man to first-team All-SEC. The Gators grab 43.1% of their missed shots (2nd in the nation). They suffocate opponents in the paint. Added guards Boogie Fland and Xaivian Lee to replace Walter Clayton Jr.

**Why Vulnerable:**
Got blown out by Vanderbilt in the SEC semis right before the tournament. Repeating is historically extremely hard — only UConn (2024) and Florida themselves (2007) have done it in recent decades. Houston is in the same region and plays at the Toyota Center (2.5 miles from campus) for the Elite Eight — massive home-court advantage for the Cougars. Guard play is still unproven.

### 6. 🔵 IOWA STATE — Midwest #2 Seed (27-8)
**Why Dangerous:**
A defensive buzzsaw. Forces turnovers at the 4th-highest rate in the country thanks to point guard Tamin Lipsey's ball pressure and elite rotation defense. Has the most favorable setup of any Iowa State team in decades for a deep run. Great early matchups and home-state fan support. Pushed Arizona to the brink in the Big 12 semis.

**Why Vulnerable:**
Moderate offense — relies heavily on the defense creating offense. Will face Michigan if both advance to the Elite Eight, and Michigan is 179th in turnovers (which is what Iowa State exploits). Odds tell the story: 16-1 to win it all, much longer than teams ranked similarly in KenPom.

### 7. 🔵 UCONN — East #2 Seed (29-5)
**Why Dangerous:**

Lost to St. John's in the Big East title game but that was an off night, not a trend. Experienced and well-coached — could capitalize if Duke's injuries catch up to them in a potential Elite Eight.

**Why Vulnerable:**
Dropped to a #2 seed after that Big East loss. If Duke is healthy, they're a level above everyone in the East. Possible path includes Michigan State and Kansas before getting to Duke.

### 8. 🔵 MICHIGAN STATE — East #3 Seed (25-8)
**Why Dangerous:**

**Why Vulnerable:**
Fears has a penchant for losing composure and getting technical fouls (he was T'd up for a groin kick during the regular season). If Fears gets into foul trouble or loses his head, Michigan State has no Plan B at the guard position. They're also on a tough path through UConn potentially.

Graham Ike: 19.7 ppg, 57% FG. Preseason #1 AP team.

Lost three of their last four regular season games before the Big Ten tournament. Big Ten tournament success could be a mirage — only played four games in quick succession. Arizona looms as a massive obstacle in the Elite Eight.

### 🎯 3-5 Contrarian Picks to Separate from the Chalk

**1. ARIZONA as Champion (not Duke or Michigan)**
- Consensus public picks will load up on Duke (+300) and Michigan (+350) as the two betting co-favorites. Arizona (+400) is being somewhat overlooked. But Arizona has the most balanced team — top-5 offense AND defense — and the tournament path through the West region, while tricky, doesn't include the same caliber of coaching gauntlet as the East (Izzo, Self, Hurley, Pitino). This is the pick that wins your office pool because everyone else is going with Michigan or Duke.

**2. HOFSTRA over ALABAMA in the first round**
- Nobody in your pool will have Hofstra. Alabama just had their #2 scorer arrested the morning after Selection Sunday. Hofstra has real size (7-foot Sunday, 6-10 Onuetu), a proven backcourt (Davis + Edmead), and road wins at ACC schools. If you're right, you're the only person in your pool with a correct 13-4 upset, which pays massive points in most bracket scoring systems.

**3. NORTHERN IOWA to the Sweet 16**
- Most people will take St. John's and move on. The data overwhelmingly supports Northern Iowa. If you advance the Panthers to the Sweet 16 and they take down Kansas (also realistic given Kansas's volatility with Peterson in/out), you're differentiated from 90%+ of all brackets in your pool.

**4. HOUSTON to the National Championship (not Florida)**
- The default "storyline" bracket has Florida repeating. But Houston is playing at home for the Elite Eight, returns their runner-up core, and adds Flemings. Picking Houston to beat Florida in the South Elite Eight is the smart contrarian play because virtually everyone defaults to the defending champion narrative.

**5. VIRGINIA as a Dark Horse Elite Eight team in the Midwest**
- Virginia at 80-1 national title odds is a joke. They're 29-5, Tony Bennett's defense could legitimately slow Michigan in a Sweet 16. They beat everyone they were supposed to beat. Taking Virginia to the Elite Eight (they lose to Michigan there) differentiates you and if Michigan somehow has an off night, you're looking at a massive upset hit. The safest contrarian pick in the bracket — high floor, surprise upside.
