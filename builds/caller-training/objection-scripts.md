# Objection Scripts — Word for Word

Every common objection you'll hit calling landscapers. Organized by bucket. Multiple variations so you don't sound like a robot.

Read these out loud. Don't just skim them. Your brain needs to hear your own voice say these before a live call.

---

## How to Use This

1. Before your first calling session, read every objection and response out loud
2. Pick 2 responses per objection that feel natural to YOU — use those
3. When you freeze on a call, figure out the bucket, come back here after and drill the one you missed
4. Never read from this during a live call — these are for practice, not scripts

---

# BUCKET 1: Not Interested (Brush-Off)

---

## "Not Interested"

**What's happening:** Reflex rejection. They said it before their brain processed you.

**Response A:**
*"Totally fair — I'll be honest, most people say that before they hear what we do. Real quick: are you happy with your lead volume going into this season, or is that something you're looking to improve?"*

**Response B:**
*"Appreciate that. One quick question before I let you go — are you guys currently getting any leads from Facebook ads, or is it all word of mouth?"*

**Response C:**
*"Got it. I'm not here to sell you something today — just want to ask one question. You good with that? [pause] Are you looking to grow this season or are you locked in on capacity?"*

---

## "We're Good, Thanks" / "We Don't Need That"

**What's happening:** Polite dismissal. They're not really saying no to your service — they're saying no to continuing the conversation.

**Response A:**
*"Good to hear. Out of curiosity — what does your lead flow look like right now? Are you guys mostly referral-based or running anything digital?"*

**Response B:**
*"That's what I like to hear. Just to understand — when you say you're good, is that more like you're at capacity, or more like the leads you are getting are quality enough?"*

**Response C:**
*"Makes sense. I won't take up much of your time — just one question: if we could put a few extra qualified jobs in your pipeline every month without any upfront cost, would that even be worth a 10-minute look?"*

---

# BUCKET 2: Timing (Not Now)

---

## "Call Me Back in Spring" / "Call Me Back Later"

**What's happening:** They're open but the timing feels off. This is actually a buying signal — treat it like one.

**Response A:**
*"I hear you — spring is the money season. That's exactly why I'm reaching out now. The guys who set this up in winter hit the ground running in April while everyone else is scrambling. Could we just do a quick 5-minute call this week so you're not starting from scratch when you're ready?"*

**Response B:**
*"Absolutely — when specifically? I want to make sure I actually follow up. Would [first week of March / April] work, or is there a better window?"*
*(Lock in a date — vague callbacks don't happen.)*

**Response C:**
*"I can do that. Before I let you go — is it more of a budget thing right now, or just not the right season to be thinking about it?"*
*(Find out why so your callback is relevant.)*

---

## "I'm Busy" / "Bad Time"

**What's happening:** Not a no — a timing signal. Could be Bucket 2 or just literal busy. Treat it like timing.

**Response A:**
*"No problem — I've literally got two minutes, or I can call you back at a specific time. Which works better?"*

**Response B:**
*"I get it — when's a better time today? I'll call you back then."*
*(Simple. Specific. Shows you respect their time.)*

**Response C:**
*"I'll keep it quick — one question and I'll let you go. Would more qualified leads this season be something worth a 5-minute conversation?"*

---

## "Not the Right Time of Year" / "Off-Season"

**What's happening:** They don't see the urgency to act now. Show them why now is actually the right time.

**Response A:**
*"Totally get it — winter's slower. That's actually why I'm calling landscapers now. Getting set up during the off-season means your ads are optimized and leads are coming in the moment spring kicks off. Would it be worth a quick call just to understand how it works?"*

**Response B:**
*"Makes sense. When does your busy season typically start? [They answer.] Perfect — so if we got everything set up [X weeks before that], you'd hit the ground running. That's the whole point of this call."*

**Response C:**
*"I hear you — I'm not asking you to sign anything today. I just want to plant a seed. Could we get 5 minutes on the calendar for when you're ready to revisit it?"*

# BUCKET 3: Trust (Who Are You / Is This Legit)

---

## "How Did You Get My Number?"

**What's happening:** Not actually hostile — they want to know you're legitimate. Answer directly.

**Response A:**
*"Business directory — same way anyone finds a landscaping company. I'm reaching out because we specifically work with landscapers, and based on your service area, I thought there might be a fit. Is that okay?"*

**Response B:**
*"We pull from public business listings — your company came up in the area we're working in. I'm not trying to be sketchy about it — straightforward question: would more qualified leads for your landscaping business even be worth a 5-minute conversation?"*

**Response C:**
*"Honestly? Same way most people find businesses — you've got a listed number. I know it feels weird getting cold calls. I'll be quick: we generate leads for landscapers on a pay-per-appointment basis. Worth hearing the quick version?"*

---

## "Send Me an Email"

**What's happening:** Classic stall tactic. Sometimes genuine, usually a polite brushoff. Don't just send the email — get a commitment.

**Response A:**
*"I can definitely do that — I want to make sure it's actually relevant to you though. Can I ask you one quick question first so I'm not sending something generic?"*
*(Ask the qualifying question → tailor the email → reference the call when sending.)*

**Response B:**
*"I'll send it — but honestly, emails like this usually get buried. Would it be more useful to just do a 5-minute call instead? I can show you the whole thing visually in less time than it takes to read an email."*

**Response C:**
*"Sure — what's the email? [Get it.] And just so you know what to look for: I'll send over how the pay-per-appointment model works with a couple of examples. When do you think you'd take a look at it?"*
*(Get a time commitment.)*

---

## "I've Never Heard of You" / "What Company Is This?"

**What's happening:** Trust gap. Give them something concrete, fast.

**Response A:**
*"Fair — we're Straight Point Marketing. We specifically work with landscaping companies running Facebook ads for lead gen. We're not a huge agency — we work with a focused group of clients and get paid per appointment we deliver. Does that make sense?"*

**Response B:**
*"We're pretty niche — we only work with landscaping companies, which is why I'm calling you specifically. The reason you haven't heard of us is we don't do a lot of outbound — we grow through referrals and cold outreach like this. Happy to tell you exactly how it works."*

---

## "I've Been Burned by Marketing Companies Before"

**What's happening:** This is a big one. Real pain point. Don't get defensive — use it.

**Response A:**
*"I hear that a lot — honestly, most marketing companies charge you whether it works or not. That's the model. We don't do that. We get paid per appointment we actually deliver. If we don't deliver, you don't pay. The risk is on us, not you."*

**Response B:**
*"That's exactly why I want to talk to you — because the way we're set up is specifically to solve that problem. No retainer, no ad spend from you, no paying for nothing. You pay per qualified appointment. Would that model at least make it worth a 5-minute conversation?"*

**Response C:**
*"What happened, if you don't mind me asking?"*
*(Let them vent. Don't interrupt. Then:)*
*"Yeah that's exactly the kind of setup we're built to be the opposite of. No upfront cost, no long contracts, pay per result. That's the only way I'd pitch this to someone who's been burned."*

---

## "Already Working With Someone" / "We Have a Lead Gen Company"

**What's happening:** Could be a genuine no (Bucket 4) OR a trust/comparison issue. Ask before assuming.

**Response A:**
*"That's good — what are they doing for you? Facebook ads, Google, something else?"*
*(Listen → find the gap → position your difference.)*

**Response B:**
*"Nice. Are you happy with the results, or is there room for improvement?"*
*(If happy → respect it, exit clean. If room to improve → "That's exactly the conversation I'd want to have.")*

**Response C:**
*"We actually work alongside other lead gen sources for a lot of our clients — we're not asking you to replace anything. Would it be worth understanding if adding a Facebook-specific channel would supplement what you're already doing?"*

# BUCKET 4: Full Objection — Specific Situations

*(These often land in Bucket 3 or 4 depending on context)*

---

## "Too Expensive" / "Not in Budget"

**What's happening:** They're evaluating value vs. cost. Reframe the model — you don't charge upfront.

**Response A:**
*"I get that — most marketing feels like a gamble. The reason I'm calling specifically is we don't charge you upfront. You only pay per appointment we deliver. So there's no budget risk — if it doesn't work, you don't pay."*

**Response B:**
*"What's your budget concern — is it cash flow right now, or you've spent money on marketing before and didn't see ROI?"*
*(Get specific. Address the actual concern.)*

**Response C:**
*"Fair. What if the cost of the service was directly tied to the results? That's how we're set up — pay per appointment, not per month. Does that change the conversation at all?"*

---

## "We Already Have Leads" / "We Get Enough Work"

**What's happening:** Satisfaction with current situation. Probe before accepting it.

**Response A:**
*"That's awesome — where are they coming from? Referrals? Google? Just curious."*
*(They answer → "Have you ever tested Facebook specifically?" → opens the door.)*

**Response B:**
*"Good problem to have. Are you turning work away right now, or is it more like you're keeping up but there's room for more?"*

**Response C:**
*"Totally respect that. Just out of curiosity — if we could add 5–10 qualified leads a month on top of what you're already getting with no upfront cost, would that be worth even hearing about?"*

# NEVER SAY THESE

This is just as important as knowing what to say.

---

**"I understand how you feel, I felt that way too, but I found..."**
→ Sounds like a script. Everyone knows this move. Never use the feel/felt/found framework.

**"I'm sorry to bother you..."**
→ Apologizing for your call signals that you think you're bothering them. Don't undermine yourself.

**"This will only take a second..."**
→ Almost always a lie. If you can't deliver in a second, don't promise it.

**"Can I be honest with you?"**
→ Implies you've been dishonest up to this point.

**"I just wanted to..."**
→ "Just" is a weak word. You're not "just" anything. You have something valuable. Act like it.

**"Are you the decision maker?"**
→ Condescending. Say: "Are you the person who handles the marketing side of things?"

**"Does that make sense?"** (repeated)
→ Once is fine. Asking it repeatedly makes you sound unsure of yourself.

**"I know you're busy, but..."**
→ If you say this, you've already told them you're about to waste their time.

**"Trust me..."**
→ Nobody says this unless they know you shouldn't. Let your content do the trusting.

**"Honestly?" / "To be honest..."** (over and over)
→ One time is fine for emphasis. Saying it on every point makes everything before it sound dishonest.
