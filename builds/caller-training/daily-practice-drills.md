# Daily Practice Drills

Do this before every calling session. No exceptions.

This isn't optional warm-up. This is the difference between your first 10 calls being sharp and your first 10 calls being you finding your footing while real prospects hang up on you.

10 minutes. Every day. Let's go.

---

# THE 10-MINUTE DAILY WARM-UP

**Total time: ~10 minutes**
Do this in order. Don't skip steps.

---

## Step 1 — Voice Warm-Up (2 min)

Your voice is your tool. Cold voice = cold call.

**Exercise:**
1. Say your opener out loud 3x at normal pace
2. Say it 1x slower than normal — feel every word
3. Say it 1x faster than normal
4. Say it 1x at the pace you actually want to use

*Opener example:* "Hey [name], this is [your name] calling from Straight Point Marketing — quick question, are you the person who handles the business side of things?"

**Then:** Say 5 words out loud, emphasizing a different word each time:
- "We only charge per APPOINTMENT."
- "We ONLY charge per appointment."
- "We only CHARGE per appointment."
- etc.

This trains you to land emphasis naturally instead of sounding robotic.

---

## Step 2 — Tone Matching Exercise (2 min)

Grab a partner (or do it solo by recording yourself). Practice matching and adjusting to 3 tones.

**Solo version:**
1. Say your opener in a Warm tone — relaxed, conversational, slight smile in your voice
2. Say your opener in a Guarded tone — flatter, more direct, less energy
3. Say your opener in a Hostile tone — wait, you don't match hostile. Practice staying calm when someone's sharp: say your opener, then immediately say the exit line calmly: "Completely understand — have a good one."

**With partner:**
Partner randomly picks a tone without telling you. They answer the phone in that tone. You identify it and respond accordingly. Switch after 2 attempts.

**Cue to lock in:** Before you hit dial, remind yourself: *"First sentence tells me everything. What tone am I hearing?"*

---

## Step 3 — Objection Bucket Rapid-Fire (3 min)

This is the core drill. The goal is to call the bucket fast — under 3 seconds.

**Solo version:** Open `reading-the-call.md`, go to the "What Bucket Is This?" drill. Cover the answer key. Read each line out loud, call the bucket, then check.

**With Andrew (or partner):**
Andrew reads lines — caller calls out the bucket.

**Format:**
> Andrew: *"Call me back in the spring."*
> Caller: *"Bucket 2."*
> Andrew: *"Correct. Next: 'Not interested.'"*
> Caller: *"Bucket 1."*

**Speed goal:** Under 2 seconds per answer. If you're hesitating, that's the one to work on.

**Variation — live lines:** Andrew makes up NEW lines not from the drill. Caller has to identify the bucket. This is the real-world version.

*Example live lines:*
- "My nephew handles all of that" → B4 (not the DM, or genuine structural)
- "We tried Google Ads last year, it was a waste" → B3 (trust)
- "I'm not really in growth mode right now" → B2 or B4 (probe before deciding)
- "Who gave you this number?" → B3

---

## Step 4 — One Objection Deep Dive (2 min)

Every day, pick ONE objection. Say the full response out loud 3x.

**How to pick:**
- If you had a calling session yesterday: pick the objection you got that you didn't handle well
- If you're starting fresh: rotate through the list in `objection-scripts.md`

**The 3x rule:**
Say it once cold. Say it again slowly. Say it a third time at conversational speed.

By the third time, it should feel natural — not read.

---

## Step 5 — Set Your Intention (1 min)

Before you dial:
1. What is your ONE focus for this session? (Example: "I'm going to stay in B2 responses longer before accepting a timing objection." or "I'm going to slow down on guarded prospects.")
2. Say it out loud.

You're not trying to fix everything in one session. Pick one thing to be intentional about. That's how you get better over time.

# 3-MINUTE ROLE PLAY FORMAT

Use this for structured role plays with Andrew or another caller.

---

## Setup

- **Caller:** Uses the script opener, responds to objections
- **Prospect (Andrew):** Plays a landscaper — picks a scenario below or makes one up
- **Timer:** 3 minutes hard cap — simulate a real short cold call

---

## Scenario Options

**Easy:** Warm prospect, one B1 brush-off, then opens up
**Medium:** Guarded prospect, B2 timing objection + B3 trust objection
**Hard:** Hostile open, B1 or B3, caller has to either recover or exit clean

---

## Pass/Fail Criteria

### PASS if the caller:
- [ ] Confirms DM in the first 20 seconds
- [ ] Keeps opener to 2–3 sentences max
- [ ] Correctly identifies the objection bucket (says it out loud after the call)
- [ ] Responds to objection with the right framework (validates → pivots → asks question)
- [ ] Doesn't apologize for calling
- [ ] Stays conversational (not robotic)
- [ ] Attempts to move toward booking before the 3 minutes is up
- [ ] Exits clean if given a Genuine No

### FAIL if the caller:
- [ ] Uses "how are you doing today" on a guarded/hostile prospect
- [ ] Re-pitches after a Genuine No
- [ ] Freezes for more than 3 seconds without recovering
- [ ] Doesn't know which bucket the objection was in (when asked after)
- [ ] Apologizes for calling
- [ ] Keeps going after the prospect signals hostile
- [ ] Tries to read from the script mid-call

---

## Debrief (after every role play)

**3 questions:**
1. What bucket was the main objection?
2. What did you do well?
3. What one thing changes in the next rep?

Keep it fast. Run 2–3 reps per session if time allows. Speed of feedback matters more than length of feedback.

# WEEKLY PROGRESSION BENCHMARKS

These are your weekly checkpoints. Be honest about where you're at.

---

## Week 1 — Foundation

**You're on track if:**
- [ ] You can name all 4 buckets without looking
- [ ] You score 12/15 or better on the bucket drill
- [ ] You score 8/10 or better on the tone drill
- [ ] You've read `objection-scripts.md` out loud at least once
- [ ] You've done the 10-minute warm-up every day before calling

**Live calling goal:** 20–30 dials/day. Focus on not freezing — not on booking. Get your sea legs.

---

## Week 2 — Handling Objections

**You're on track if:**
- [ ] You hit 15/15 on the bucket drill consistently
- [ ] You have 2 responses memorized for every objection in `objection-scripts.md`
- [ ] You've correctly handled B1 and B2 objections in live calls (Andrew confirms)
- [ ] You can role play Scenario: Easy and Scenario: Medium and pass both
- [ ] You've annotated at least 3 of your own calls using the template

**Live calling goal:** 30+ dials/day. Start tracking: how many conversations actually happened?

---

## Week 3 — Independent Operation

**You're on track if:**
- [ ] You pass all 3 role play scenarios (Easy/Medium/Hard) in one session
- [ ] You've booked at least 2 qualified calls in live sessions
- [ ] You can handle B3 trust objections smoothly without Andrew coaching on the spot
- [ ] Your connect-to-conversation rate is improving vs. Week 1
- [ ] You can self-annotate a call and identify your own miss without Andrew pointing it out

**Live calling goal:** 40+ dials/day. Quality of conversations mattering as much as volume now.

---

## Week 4 — Fully Independent

**You're ready to operate without Andrew on the phones when:**
- [ ] Booking rate is consistent — at least 1–2 bookings per calling session
- [ ] No call requires post-call coaching on the same objection twice in a row
- [ ] You've passed all 3 role play scenarios in back-to-back sessions
- [ ] You're annotating your own calls and fixing your own misses
- [ ] Tone identification is automatic — you're adjusting in real time, not after

## When You Have a Bad Session

Every caller has bad sessions. The move is to figure out what went wrong — not to feel bad about it.

After a bad session, do this:
1. Pull your worst call — annotate it
2. Identify the objection you got that you didn't handle
3. Find the response in `objection-scripts.md`
4. Say it out loud 5x before you go home

Come back the next day ready to handle that exact situation differently.

That's the job. Put in the reps.
