# Call Annotation Guide

This is for Andrew when reviewing recordings, and for callers reviewing their own calls. Breaking down a call properly is how you go from "I had a bad call" to "I know exactly what went wrong and how to fix it."

Don't just listen to calls and have a vibe about them. Use this system.

---

## Why Annotate Calls

Most callers listen to recordings and either cringe or feel good about themselves. Neither is useful.

Annotation forces you to slow down and actually identify:
- What objection came up and what bucket it was
- What tone the prospect was in
- What the caller did (vs. what they should have done)
- The specific moment the call turned

You fix the specific moment — not "the whole call."

---

## The Annotation Template

Use this for every recording you review. Takes 5–10 minutes per call.

## What Goes In Each Column

**Timestamp:** Rough time marker — doesn't need to be exact, just enough to find it again

**What Happened:** One sentence. Not an essay. "Prospect said not interested." "Caller got flustered." "Owner started asking questions."

**Bucket / Tone:** The bucket of the objection (B1/B2/B3/B4) and the prospect's tone (Warm/Guarded/Hostile). Put N/A if it's not an objection moment.

**What Caller Did:** What actually happened. Be specific. "Caller said 'I understand' and re-pitched the whole service."

**What Should Have Happened:** What the move was. "Should have identified as B1, asked one qualifying question, stayed conversational."

## Annotated Call Transcript #1 — Caller Handles It Well

**Context:** Cold call to a mid-sized landscaping company in Ohio. Owner answers.

---

**[0:00–0:15] — Opener**

> Owner: "Yeah, this is Mike."
> Caller: "Hey Mike, this is Jake calling from Straight Point Marketing. Quick question — are you the guy who handles the business side of things for your landscaping company?"
> Owner: "Yeah, that's me."

**Bucket/Tone:** N/A — Warm (relaxed answer, confirmed quickly)

**What Caller Did:** Asked a confirming question rather than launching into a pitch. Kept it short and casual.

**What Should Have Happened:** ✅ This is exactly right. Confirming the DM early, not going into the pitch until you know you're talking to the right person.

---

**[0:15–0:45] — Value Statement + First Objection**

> Caller: "Perfect. I'll keep it quick — we run Facebook lead gen for landscaping companies on a pay-per-appointment basis. Only charge when we deliver. Wanted to see if it'd make sense for your operation."
> Owner: "We're actually pretty good right now — got enough work coming in."

**Bucket/Tone:** B1 (brush-off) / Warm

**What Caller Did:** Paused briefly, then said: "Good to hear — where's most of it coming from right now? Referrals, Google, what's working for you?"

**What Should Have Happened:** ✅ Nailed it. B1 response — didn't fight the objection, asked a genuine question, turned it into a conversation. The prospect just told him he's open (warm tone + B1 brush-off = still in it).

---

**[0:45–1:30] — Prospect Opens Up**

> Owner: "Mostly referrals, some Nextdoor stuff. We haven't really done paid ads."
> Caller: "Nice — referrals are the best kind of lead. Question: are you happy where you're at for the season, or would adding a few more qualified jobs a month be something worth looking at?"
> Owner: "I mean... yeah, we're always looking to grow. Just haven't found something that works."

**Bucket/Tone:** Warm — getting warmer

**What Caller Did:** Used a genuine curiosity question, stayed low-pressure, let the owner answer. Picked up on "haven't found something that works" and held it.

**What Should Have Happened:** ✅ Perfect. This is the moment the prospect self-identified as a buyer. Caller let him get there on his own.

---

**[1:30–2:00] — Trust Objection**

> Owner: "I've tried running my own ads before. Didn't really go anywhere."
> Caller: "Yeah that's pretty common — running ads yourself is hard, especially if it's not your main thing. The difference with us is we only get paid per appointment we actually deliver. So if it doesn't work, you don't pay. The risk is on us."
> Owner: "How does that work exactly?"

**Bucket/Tone:** B3 (trust — past bad experience) / Warm

**What Caller Did:** Acknowledged the past experience without being dismissive, pivoted to the pay-per-result model, and let it land.

**What Should Have Happened:** ✅ Correct response. He didn't defend marketing broadly, he reframed specifically on their model. When the owner asked "how does that work" — the call is now officially on.

---

**[2:00–2:45] — Booking**

> Caller: "So we'd set up ads targeting homeowners in your service area who are actively looking for landscaping. Leads come in, we qualify them, you only pay per appointment booked. My boss Andrew runs all the strategy — would it be worth a 15-minute call with him to see if it's a fit for your area?"
> Owner: "Sure, I can do that. What's the process?"
> Caller: "Just need a couple of your availability windows — he works around your schedule."

**Bucket/Tone:** N/A — warm close

**What Caller Did:** Clean, low-pressure close. Got the yes, moved to logistics immediately.

**What Should Have Happened:** ✅ Good. One small note — "15 minutes" is better than "quick call" because it's specific and signals you respect their time.

**Overall Assessment:**
- Strongest moment: Turning B1 brush-off into a conversation with one question
- Biggest miss: None — solid call
- One thing to build on: Could have gotten more specific on seasonality to create urgency

**Rating: Pass ✅**

## Annotated Call Transcript #2 — Caller Misses It

**Context:** Cold call to a landscaping owner in Texas. He answers immediately, sounds clipped.

---

**[0:00–0:20] — Opener**

> Owner: "Yeah?"
> Caller: "Hey, how are you doing today? This is Tyler calling from Straight Point Marketing — I was hoping to speak with the owner of the landscaping company?"
> Owner: "That's me. What do you want?"

**Bucket/Tone:** N/A — **Guarded** (one-word answer, clipped question)

**What Caller Did:** Used "how are you doing today" opener — social pleasantry on a cold call. Owner responds with "what do you want" — annoyance is already up.

**What Should Have Happened:** Skip the pleasantry. Guarded tone = get to the point fast. Better opener: *"Hey — are you the guy who handles the business side of the landscaping operation?"* — direct, confirms DM, no filler.

---

**[0:20–0:50] — Value Statement**

> Caller: "Great. So we work with landscaping companies across the US, and we've been helping them generate leads through Facebook ads, and we have a really unique pay-per-appointment model that's been really successful for our clients, and I wanted to see if that was something you'd be interested in learning more about..."
> Owner: "Not interested."

**Bucket/Tone:** B1 — Guarded

**What Caller Did:** Long run-on pitch with "really, really, really" in it. Lost the prospect's attention before he even got to the value prop. When the B1 came, the caller got flustered.

**What Should Have Happened:** Tighten the pitch to two sentences max with a guarded prospect. *"We run Facebook lead gen for landscapers — only charge per appointment we deliver. Does that sound like something worth 5 minutes of your time?"* Then wait.

---

**[0:50–1:20] — Mishandles B1**

> Caller: "Oh — well, I just want to say that I totally understand you're not interested, but if you'd just give me a few minutes, I think you'd really see the value in what we offer and I promise it's worth your time..."
> Owner: "I said not interested." *[hangs up]*

**Bucket/Tone:** B1 → transitioning to Hostile

**What Caller Did:** Apologetic re-pitch. Said "I just want to say," "I totally understand," "I promise" — all weak signals. Basically begged for time instead of creating interest.

**What Should Have Happened:** One clean attempt to re-engage, then exit if he shuts it down again.

Better response to the B1: *"Fair enough — quick question before I let you go: is it more that leads aren't a priority right now, or you've tried this kind of thing before and it didn't work out?"*

If he says not interested again → *"Got it. Appreciate your time. Have a good one."* Hang up.

---

**Overall Assessment:**
- Strongest moment: Actually confirmed he was the owner — good
- Biggest misses:
  1. Social opener on a guarded prospect immediately put him on defense
  2. Pitch was too long and too vague for someone who's clearly not giving much
  3. B1 response sounded desperate — re-pitched instead of pivoting
  4. Kept going after second "not interested" instead of exiting clean
- One thing to fix before next session: Identify tone in the first 5 seconds and adjust energy down for guarded prospects

**Rating: Needs Work ⚠️**

## Tips for Doing Your Own Annotations

**Do this within 2 hours of the call.** The longer you wait, the less you remember about what you were thinking in the moment.

**Be honest.** The goal isn't to feel good. The goal is to get better. If you messed it up, say you messed it up — that's what the annotation is for.

**One fix at a time.** Identify ONE thing to fix. Not seven. One. Fix it before the next session.

**Look for patterns.** If you're annotating 3 calls in a row and the same miss shows up — that's your thing to work on this week.
