> "We run Facebook and Instagram ads for your landscaping company, handle all the lead qualification calls in-house, and guarantee you a set number of qualified appointments — or we refund you for every one we miss."

## SALES CALL STRUCTURE (15 minutes)

### Min 0–2: Warm Up
- "How's business going right now — busy season starting to pick up?"
- "Are you running any ads currently or mostly word of mouth?"
- Goal: Get them talking. Understand their current situation.

### Min 2–5: Pain Discovery
- "What's your biggest challenge right now when it comes to getting new customers?"
- "Have you tried any paid ads or lead gen before — what happened?"
- "If you could add 10 solid customers this month, what would that mean for the business?"
- Goal: Identify the gap between where they are and where they want to be.

### Min 5–10: The Pitch
> "Here's what we do — we run Facebook and Instagram ads specifically targeting homeowners in your service area who are actively looking for landscaping work right now. Every lead that comes in, we call them ourselves first. We check: do they have a budget, are they ready to hire soon, are they in your area. If they pass, that's the appointment you get. You never talk to someone who's just kicking tires.
>
> We guarantee 10 qualified appointments in your first 30 days. If we fall short, you get $100 back for every appointment we miss. No long-term contract on the starter package.
>
> The investment is $1,000 for the month."

### Min 10–13: Handle Questions
*(See objection guide below)*

### Min 13–15: Close
> "So here's how I'd put it — you're spending $1,000 for a guaranteed shot at 10 new customers. If even 3 of those close, at whatever your average job size is, you've already made your money back. Most landscapers we work with are closing 4–6 out of 10. Does that math work for you?"

*(If yes → payment + onboarding)*
*(If hesitating → see objections)*

## OBJECTIONS ON THE SALES CALL

### "I need to think about it"
> "I totally get it — what's the main thing holding you back? Is it the timing, the budget, or just not sure if it'll work?"
> *(Identify the specific objection and address it directly. "I need to think about it" is rarely the real objection.)*

### "It's too expensive"
> "I hear you. Here's how I'd think about it: what's your average job size?"
> *(Wait for answer — typically $500–$5,000 for a landscaping job)*
> "So if we book 10 appointments and you close 3 — which is conservative — that's [3x their job size] in revenue from a $1,000 investment. The risk isn't in trying it; the risk is in not having a predictable way to grow. Does that math make sense?"

### "I've been burned by marketing agencies before"
> "What happened? I want to understand."
> *(Listen fully — don't interrupt)*
> "That's fair. The difference here is we don't just run ads and hand you leads — we call every single lead before you ever talk to them. And we guarantee appointments, not clicks or impressions. If you don't get 10 qualified calls, you get money back. We eat the risk, not you."

### "I'm not sure I have capacity right now"
> "That's actually a great problem — means you're doing something right. A lot of guys come to us because they want to be more selective: stop taking small jobs, focus on bigger tickets. We can target exactly the kind of customer you want — higher-end neighborhoods, bigger projects. Would that change how you're thinking about it?"

### "Can I start next month?"
> "Totally — I can hold a spot. I do want to be upfront: spring is the highest-performing season for landscaping ads. Homeowners are actively planning right now. Starting in April still works well, but March tends to get the best results. Is there anything that would make it possible to start this week?"

### "Do you guarantee results?"
> "Yes — 10 qualified appointments or we refund $100 for each one we miss. In writing. That's the deal."

## POSITIONING NOTES

### Why we win against other agencies:
1. **We call the leads** — they don't. Big differentiator.
2. **Guaranteed appointments** — not "we'll try our best"
3. **We know landscaping** — niche expertise vs. generalist
4. **No long-term lock-in** on starter (removes risk)

### Why landscapers buy in spring:
- Q2 is peak season — they want the pipeline full NOW
- Spring cleaning, mulch, installations = highest demand
- Budget is usually available coming off a decent fall

## FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE (Post-Call, No Decision)
*Use in GHL or manually. For prospects who didn't close on the call.*

**Day 1 (same day):**
> "Hey [FirstName], really enjoyed the call today. Sending over our case study so you have something to reference — [link]. Let me know if any questions come up. Happy to answer them by text if that's easier."

**Day 2:**
> "Hey [FirstName] — had a quick thought. You mentioned [specific pain point from call]. That's actually one of the most common things we solve. Want me to show you a quick example from a similar company in [State]?"

**Day 4:**
> "Hey [FirstName], just following up one more time. We have room for one more client in [City] area this month. If it makes sense, I'd love to get you started before the spring rush hits. If timing isn't right, totally get it — just let me know either way so I can plan accordingly."

**Day 7:**
> "Hey [FirstName] — last follow-up from me. If you ever want to revisit, I'm here. Hope spring is treating you well."
