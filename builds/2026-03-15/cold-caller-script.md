# Cold Caller Script — Straight Point Marketing
**Service:** Paid lead generation for landscaping companies (Meta ads + in-house qual calls)
**Offer:** 10 qualified appointments in 30 days for $1,000 (guaranteed or money back per appt missed)
**Last updated:** 2026-03-15

---

## Before You Dial — Know This

**Qualified appointment = prospect who has:**
- Budget (they pay for landscaping services)
- Timeline (wants work done soon, not "maybe next year")
- Location (within the client's service area)

**Your only job on the call:** Get them curious and book a 15-minute call with Andrew.

---

## THE SCRIPT

### Opening (The Hook)
> "Hey, is this [First Name]?"
> *(wait for yes)*
> "Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Straight Point Marketing. I work with landscaping companies across [State/Region] — I won't take much of your time. I'm actually calling because we just ran a campaign for a company similar to yours and got them [X] qualified customer appointments in 30 days. I wanted to see if that's something you'd be open to hearing more about — does that sound interesting at all?"

**Why this works:** Short, specific, social proof, soft ask — not a pitch dump.

---

### If YES / "Tell me more"
> "Perfect. So basically what we do is run Facebook and Instagram ads targeted to homeowners in your service area who are actively looking for landscaping right now. And before we ever pass a lead to you, we call them ourselves to make sure they have a budget, they're ready to move, and they're in your area. So you're not wasting time on tire-kickers — you only talk to people who actually want work done.
>
> We have a package where we guarantee 10 qualified appointments in 30 days, and if we fall short, you get money back for every appointment we miss. The investment is $1,000 for the month.
>
> The next step would just be a quick 15-minute call with my guy Andrew — he runs the campaigns. He'll show you exactly how it works and tell you if it's a fit for your market. What does your week look like?"

**→ BOOK THE CALL. Get a day + time. Don't negotiate price on the phone.**

---

### OBJECTIONS

---

#### "I'm not interested"
> "Totally fair — can I ask why? Is it because you're already maxed out on work, or is it more like you've tried marketing stuff before and it didn't work?"
>
> *(If maxed on work):*
> "Oh got it, that's actually a good problem to have. I'll leave you alone — but if things slow down or you want to grow the team, we'd be a good fit. Can I call you back in 60 days?"
>
> *(If bad marketing experience):*
> "Yeah that's the most common thing I hear — a lot of guys pay for leads that go nowhere. That's actually why we qualify every lead ourselves before it ever hits your calendar. It's a different model. Would it be worth just 15 minutes with Andrew to show you how we do it differently?"

---

#### "I already have enough work"
> "That's great, honestly. That means you're doing something right. A lot of guys we talk to actually want to start being more selective — only taking on the bigger jobs, raising their prices. We can help with that too — we can target higher-ticket customers in your area. Still worth a quick call?"

---

#### "How much does it cost?"
> "Great question — I don't want to give you a number without context because it depends on your market and what kind of volume you're after. That's exactly what the 15-minute call is for. Andrew will walk you through what makes sense for your situation. Are you open to that?"
>
> *(If they push):*
> "The starting package is $1,000 for 10 guaranteed qualified appointments. That's it — no retainer, no long-term contract. If we don't deliver, you get money back. But Andrew can walk you through it better than I can — when are you free?"

---

#### "Send me something / Send me an email"
> "Sure, I can do that — I just want to make sure you actually get something relevant to your market, not just a generic deck. Can I set up 15 minutes with Andrew first so he knows what to customize? Even just this week sometime?"
>
> *(If they still want email first):*
> "Totally. What's your email? I'll send our case study and a quick overview. I'll follow up in a couple days — is Tuesday or Wednesday better for a quick call after you've had a chance to look?"

---

#### "I use [other marketing company]"
> "Nice, are they doing Meta ads specifically or more like SEO/Google stuff?"
>
> *(Listen to answer — if not Meta/lead gen):*
> "Got it — we're actually doing something different. We run paid ads specifically to people actively searching for landscaping right now, and we call every single lead to qualify them before you ever hear from them. It's a different lane. Would it be worth a quick comparison call just to see how we stack up?"
>
> *(If they're already using Meta ads):*
> "Interesting — how's the lead quality been? Are you getting people who are actually ready to hire, or more tire-kickers?"
> *(Use their pain point to pivot.)*

---

#### "I'm busy right now / bad time"
> "No problem at all — when's a better time? I can call you back this afternoon or tomorrow morning — what works?"
> *(Always re-schedule immediately. Never just say "I'll try again later.")*

---

#### "How'd you get my number?"
> "We actually pull business listings from public directories — same as any business outreach. We work specifically with landscaping companies so we make sure we're reaching out to the right people. Is there a better number or time to reach you?"

---

### VOICEMAIL SCRIPT
*(Leave if no answer on 1st or 2nd try — NOT every attempt)*

> "Hey [First Name], this is [Your Name] from Straight Point Marketing. We work with landscaping companies on lead generation — I wanted to see if getting more qualified customers this spring was something you'd be interested in. Give me a call back at [YOUR NUMBER] or I'll try you again tomorrow. Talk soon."

**Keep it under 20 seconds. Don't pitch the whole thing.**

---

### BOOKING CONFIRMATION
When you get a YES to a call:

> "Perfect. Let me get this locked in. The call will be with Andrew — it's 15 minutes, totally casual, just to see if it makes sense. Do you prefer morning or afternoon? And is [Day] or [Day] better?"

*(Get a day + time → confirm it back to them → tell them they'll get a text/email reminder)*

> "I'll send you a confirmation. Just so you have it, Andrew's number is [NUMBER] if anything comes up. Looking forward to connecting you two."

---

## CALL CADENCE (per lead)
| Attempt | Timing | Action |
|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Day 1, morning | Call, no VM |
| 2 | Day 1, afternoon | Call + voicemail if no answer |
| 3 | Day 2, morning | Call, no VM |
| 4 | Day 3 | Call + voicemail |
| 5 | Day 5 | Final call — offer to text instead |
| Done | Day 5+ | Mark dead in CRM if no response |

---

## WHAT TO LOG IN GHL (EVERY CALL)
- Disposition: Answered / No Answer / VM Left / Hung Up / Interested / Booked / Dead
- Notes: What they said, objection used, best time to call back
- Next step date: Set follow-up task IMMEDIATELY

---

## DON'T DO
- ❌ Don't pitch for more than 2 minutes before asking for the meeting
- ❌ Don't negotiate price on the call — that's Andrew's job
- ❌ Don't leave a voicemail every single attempt
- ❌ Don't mark a lead dead without 5 attempts
- ❌ Don't improvise the close — use the script until it's natural

---

## DAILY TARGETS
| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Dials | 80–100 |
| Contacts (live answers) | 15–25 |
| Interested / wants info | 3–5 |
| Booked calls | 1–2 |

**1-2 booked calls per day per caller = 3-5 calls/week = hitting the pipeline goal.**
