# SMS Outreach Sequence — Landscaper Cold Outreach
**Platform:** Warmed SMS account (1,500 msg/day capacity)
**Target:** Mid-sized landscaping company owners (US-wide, city by city)
**Goal:** Book a 15-minute call with Andrew
**Last updated:** 2026-03-15

---

## OVERVIEW

5-touch sequence over 10 days. Conversational tone — NOT a blast message.
Every message should feel like it came from a real person who did 30 seconds of research.

Personalization tokens: `[FirstName]`, `[City]`, `[CompanyName]` (use what you have)

---

## SEQUENCE

---

### Message 1 — Day 1 (Initial Outreach)
**Send time:** 9:00–11:00 AM local (avoid Monday mornings)

> "Hey [FirstName] — saw [CompanyName] does landscaping in [City]. Quick question — are you actively trying to grow your customer base right now, or are you pretty maxed out?"

**Why this works:**
- Starts with a question, not a pitch
- Invites a YES or NO — either opens a conversation
- "Maxed out" framing respects their time and pre-handles an objection

---

### Message 2 — Day 2 (If No Reply)
**Send time:** 10:00 AM–12:00 PM

> "Hey [FirstName], wanted to follow up real quick. We run Facebook ads for landscaping companies and handle all the lead qualification in-house — so you only get calls from people who actually have a budget and are ready to hire. Generated 10+ appointments for a company in [City] last month. Worth a 15-min call to see if it makes sense for you?"

**Why this works:**
- Reveals the offer clearly now that they didn't bite on curiosity
- Social proof with specificity (city-level resonates with local business owners)
- Soft CTA — "worth a 15-min call" is low-commitment

---

### Message 3 — Day 4 (Value Add / Urgency)
**Send time:** 11:00 AM–1:00 PM

> "One more thing [FirstName] — spring is when landscaping leads are highest quality. Homeowners are actively budgeting right now. Most of our clients see their best ROI kicking off in March/April. Happy to save a spot for [City] if you want to chat this week."

**Why this works:**
- Seasonal urgency is REAL — not manufactured
- "Save a spot for [City]" implies exclusivity (we only work one landscaper per market)
- Doesn't re-pitch everything — adds new info

---

### Message 4 — Day 7 (Soft Last Chance)
**Send time:** 9:00–10:00 AM

> "Hey [FirstName] — I know this is the third time I've reached out, so I'll keep it short. We guarantee 10 qualified appointments in 30 days for $1,000 — or money back per appointment we miss. No long contract. If that's something you'd be open to hearing more about, I can send you a quick overview. If not, totally get it and I won't bug you again."

**Why this works:**
- Acknowledges the follow-ups (honesty builds trust)
- States the offer with the guarantee — removes risk
- Clear opt-out: "won't bug you again" — respects them, reduces blocks

---

### Message 5 — Day 10 (Final Touch / Breakup)
**Send time:** 10:00 AM–12:00 PM
**Only send if zero response to all 4 previous messages**

> "Hey [FirstName], last message from me. If timing's just not right — no worries at all. We work specifically with landscapers in [City/Region] and tend to be a good fit when companies are ready to scale. If that changes, feel free to reach back out. Take care and hope business is going well this spring 🌱"

**Why this works:**
- Leaves the door open without being desperate
- Ends positively — they'll remember you weren't annoying
- Emoji is intentional — landscaping/spring = natural fit, humanizes the message

## RESPONSE HANDLING GUIDE

| They Say | You Say |
|----------|---------|
| "Not interested" | "No problem at all — can I ask why? Just trying to understand if it's timing or if paid ads in general haven't worked for you?" |
| "How much?" | "Starting at $1,000 for 10 guaranteed appointments. But the call is really just to see if it makes sense for your market — Andrew can break it all down. When are you free?" |
| "Tell me more" | "Perfect — honestly easiest to just hop on a 15-min call. When works for you this week?" → Send Calendly or booking link |
| "We already have leads" | "That's great — are they consistent? The guys we work with usually like us because it's predictable — same volume every month. Worth a quick comparison?" |
| "Text me the info" | "Totally — sending now. [Send 1-2 sentence version of offer + link to case study or deck]. Quick call would be even faster — when are you free?" |
| "Who is this?" | "This is [Your Name] from Straight Point Marketing — we do lead gen for landscaping companies. Did I catch you at a bad time?" |
| "Remove me from your list" | "Got it — sorry to bother you, won't reach out again. Hope business is going well!" → Mark DNC in GHL immediately |

---

## DO NOT CONTACT (DNC) RULES
- Any reply that says "stop," "remove me," "don't text me" → DNC immediately in GHL
- Any number that hard-blocks → DNC
- Don't re-add DNC numbers to future campaigns

## OPTIMIZATION NOTES

### A/B Test These Variables
- **Day 1 opener:** Curiosity question (current) vs. direct benefit lead
- **Timing:** 9am vs. 11am first message
- **Day 4 message:** Exclusivity angle vs. social proof (client result)

### Track These Metrics Per Campaign (weekly)
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Response rate | Replies / Total sent | >5% |
| Positive response rate | Interested replies / Total sent | >2% |
| Book rate | Calls booked / Responses | >20% |
| DNC/block rate | DNC + blocks / Sent | <1% |

### City Targeting Priority (Spring 2026)
High-value markets for landscaping:
- Chicago suburbs (IL)
- Dallas/Fort Worth metro (TX)
- Phoenix metro (AZ)
- Atlanta suburbs (GA)
- Charlotte/Raleigh (NC)
- Denver suburbs (CO)

Avoid: Very rural markets, dense urban cores (less residential landscaping demand)

---

## SENDING GUIDELINES
- Max 300 messages/hour (stay under warmup thresholds)
- Pause campaigns on Sundays and holidays
- Never send before 8:30 AM or after 6:00 PM local time
- Monitor block rate daily — if >0.5% on a batch, pause and audit copy
